History

River rafting , also known worldwide as rafting , is a sporting and recreational activity that consists of travelling down a river in the direction of the current (downstream), usually on some type of boat or raft . Typically, the rivers that are navigated have some degree of turbulence , and are also called "whitewater" rivers because this color is characteristic of the foam generated by the turbulence in the bodies of water. Another common name for this type of river is simply " rapids ." Descent of the Lao River in Calabria , Italy The most common boats used are the raft, canoe or kayak , which can be rigid or inflatable
